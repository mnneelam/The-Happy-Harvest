Plan Your Root Garden: Friendly Steps for Every Beginner

1. Setup Basics

  • Use deep containers (12-18 in) with drainage.
  • Fill with fluffy soil and compost, never packed dirt.
  • Give 6+ hours of sun or bright grow-light time.
  • Cool days suit roots; shield pots during heat waves.
  • Water when top inch feels dry; feed monthly organically.

2. What to Grow by Season

  • Cool spring/fall: carrots, radishes, beets, turnips.
  • Mild weather: onions and beets thrive steadily.
  • Warm season: potatoes and onions in larger pots.
  • Quick wins for beginners: radishes and baby turnips.

3. Tips, Pitfalls, Best Practices

  • Thin seedlings early so roots can size up.
  • Do not overwater; soggy soil can cause rot.
  • Turn containers weekly for even light.
  • Label dates, then harvest on time for best flavor.
